Leadership

Craig Dunkin
As the Chief Executive Officer of W.R. Dunkin & Son, Inc., Craig is the fourth generation of his family to lead the company. During high school and college, Craig worked in the field as a laborer and then a brick mason.
After graduation from college, Craig spent three years in the field before joining his father in the office handling project estimating.
Today, Craig provides servies in pre-construction panning, value engineering and still puts the final touches on most of the comapny's estimates, while keeping up to date with the management of each current project
The community recognized Craig in 2007 as the first receipient of the Character in Business Award present by the The Center for Character Development and The Chamber of Commerce of Anderson and Madison County.

Skip Watkins
Skip has over 30 years experience in commercial construction. Starting as a laborer and carpenter, he eventually advanced to construction superintendent. In recent years, Skip has moved into a management role utilizing his vast knowledge of field and coordination processes.
Skip has a proven record of completing projects on time and under budget and is a strong leader, able to motivate others to achieve optimal production.
One of Skip's strongest qualities is his ability to identify and solve problems quickly. He will serve as Project Manager for the Hamilton Height Grade Reconfiguration.

About

The W.R. Dunkin and Son company strives to be strategic partners in the construction process, not just contractors. We believe in a collaborative approach with the customer and architect to work as a team in setting and achieving goals for each project. With a commitment to excellence and precision, our responsive and skilled employees are proud of the long history of success that proceeds them. They look forward to a bright future, and are dedicated to building on a foundation that was firmly put in place over 100 years ago.
